Research

Cross-layer research, from silicon to large models

We build computing systems that are dependable under faults, efficient under tight energy budgets, and intelligent by borrowing principles from the brain. Four pillars, one integrated stack. 🔬

01 🛡️

Robust & Resilient AI

Hardware faults Adversarial ML

Deep models are increasingly deployed on unreliable hardware and exposed to adversarial inputs. We study how bit-flips, timing errors, and crafted perturbations degrade accuracy — and design fault-aware training and selective protection schemes that keep networks dependable at minimal overhead.

  • Criticality-aware ECC for quantized weights
  • Resilient accelerator architectures
  • Certified robustness under combined HW + adversarial faults
02 ⚡

Hardware Reliability & Approximate Computing

DEVoT LEVAX

Energy is the binding constraint at the edge. We trade a little accuracy for large energy savings through approximate arithmetic and voltage overscaling, then recover quality with hardware-aware retraining — mapping the full energy–accuracy Pareto frontier.

  • Approximate multiplier design-space exploration
  • Voltage/timing-error modeling for accelerators
  • Quality-configurable inference
03 🐛

Fuzz Testing

Coverage-guided HW · SW · AI

Bugs hide at the seams between hardware, firmware, software, and AI runtimes. We develop structure-aware, coverage-guided fuzzers and differential oracles that surface crashes and silent correctness bugs across the whole stack.

  • Fuzzing LLM inference runtimes
  • Firmware & hardware-model fuzzing
  • Unified fault-injection + fuzzing harnesses
04 🧠 ★ FEATURED

Brain-Inspired Hyperdimensional Computing

MoleHD WIRED-featured

Inspired by the brain's high-dimensional representations, hyperdimensional computing (HDC) encodes data into long hypervectors that are single-pass trainable, noise-tolerant, and energy-frugal. Our work spans drug discovery, genomics, and NLP — and was featured in WIRED.

  • MoleHD: molecular property prediction
  • HDC for genomic sequence classification
  • Co-designed approximate HDC accelerators
